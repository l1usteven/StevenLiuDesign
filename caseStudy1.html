Weigh In

Spec Project

User Experience Design| User Interface Design | User Research

Overview

Project Brief:

Weigh In: Mobile fitness app concept idea that helps struggling gym goers find programs and exercises and tracks internally.

Time:

2 Weeks

The Problem:

Primary: Many of the users' pain points come from tracking their workouts. A main issue is the difficulty of tracking accurately when changing weights in-between sets.

Secondary: Often times when users get home from a day's work, they do not always want to have to think about what or how they should work out. They just want to follow something and that is it.

The Goal:

Objective was to implement a solution into a gym goers' workout routine through an app.

Team:

Solo Project

Responsibilities:

Quantitative/Qualitative Research, UX/UI Design, Visual Design, Prototyping, Testing, and Iterations

Technologies Used:

Concepts (iOS), FigJam, Figma, Trello, Google Drive

The Process

My process differs from project to project depending on different determining factors but not limited to:

Business needs, project goals, user needs, complexity, time and other constraints.

Research

User Surveys:

I surveyed users to determine the best suited candidates to interview and to gain some initial insights.

Survey Results:

Do you track your workouts?

Do you have your phone on you while you workout?

How long do your workouts last?

User Interviews:

Objective: To understand the pain points when trying to find a workout routine and tracking their workouts.

I started off the interview by providing the interviewees with a brief overview so they have a bit more context to what the interview was going to be about. I then moved on to framing questions as a way to get them to open up about their past experiences with their workout routine and tracking their progress. Afterwards, I utilized my general questions in order to touch on any specific points I thought could round out the rest of their interview with.

Research Analysis:

In order to actually make sense of all the data collected from surveys and interviews, I organized all the data with an affinity map.

Research Synthesis:

By utilizing the affinity map, I was able to categorize key takeaways from the user interview and later develop a persona that best fit the target user. Meet, Marty.

Persona:

Name: Marty

Gender: Male

Occupation: Lawyer

Archetype: Gym Rat

Bio:

Marty is the strong but silent type who works out almost every day. He enjoys listening to hip-hop with headphones on at the gym to block out any distractions. His day job as a lawyer forces him to go to the gym later at night when most of the regular gym goers are home already. With such a busy lifestyle, he struggles to find and maintain workout programs that fit his schedule. Even when he does, it is even harder for him to keep track of everything.

Goals:

  • Easily find workout programs
  • Track every detail of his workout

Needs:

  • Find workouts that align with his busy work schedule
  • Simple ways to track every detail of his workout progress

Pain Points:

  • Workout programs that are too complicated and long
  • Tracking apps that do not track everything that he is looking for

Ideation

Brain Dump:

When starting out my ideation phase, I like to ust throw everything that comes to min onto paper or on my iPad. This allows me to visually see what I have in mind for a specific feature or just a list of points I want to prioritize. From there, I can pick and choose which aspects of the design that I like and can build off of. Ultimately, this allows me to be more efficient and focus the majority of my time on the later steps.

Focus:

  1. Tracking weight changes during a workout
  2. Finding workout plants within the app

Testing

After designing the initial prototype, I conducted rounds of usability testing in order to best guide later iteration changes.

Usability Testing:

I created a couple tasks for the users to complete in order to gauge their perception of the design.

Key Takeaways:

  1. Users were confused by the initial filter menu button.
  2. Users did not know if their actions were performed after clicking some menu buttons.
  3. Users were confused if they reached their last workout.

Iteration:

Taking the information gathered from the usability testing, I iterated on my design choices to make the user experience more intuitive.

  1. Changed to the appropriate filter icon.
  2. Pop up messages were added.
  3. Title of the screen was changed and pop up messages were added.

Reflection

Challenges:

The most challenging part of this project has to be the usability testing. At first, I found it difficult to get the testers to speak more openly. After doing a couple more tests, I was able to get a better grasp on what and how to orient my questions in order for the testers to open up about their experience testing the app.

Next Steps:

  1. Develop a more appealing notification system.
  2. Create a better information architecture to make it easier for users to search for workout programs.
  3. Create a full iOS, WatchOS, and MacOS app suite.
