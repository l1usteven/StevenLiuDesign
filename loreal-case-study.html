    Overview

    The L'Oreal Shade Finder AI helps customers navigate the complexity of finding the perfect foundation shade for their unique skin tone. YSL has expressed interest in integrating this tool into their in-store experience, with hopes to include recommendations for complementary makeup products.

    During this project, my responsibility was to create a proof-of-concept (POC) app and a visual mockup of the final product. The project lasted around one month, with intermittent periods of work.

    Problems

    L'Oreal

    Inefficient & Inconsistent

    The current testing methodology for the algorithm was both inefficient and inconsistent, making it challenging to continually enhance it.

    Yves Saint Laurent

    Upsell Potential

    YSL sought additional methods to suggest products that would complement their clients' makeup regimen, utilizing data from the Shade Finder algorithm.

    Solutions

    L'Oreal

    Active User Feedback

    Implemented a simple and efficient survey to gather user feedback on recommendations.

    Yves Saint Laurent

    Color Story & Complexion Routine

    "Color Story" recommended products based on color theory to attain a specific look, while "Complexion Routine" focused on products to attain an even overall skin tone.

    User Flow

    The app will be used by Beauty Assistants (BAs) during in-store customer interactions. To fully understand their usage of the app, I created a user flow that mirrors their navigation within the app.

    Shade Finder App User Flow

    Iterative Prototyping

    With the essential requirements in mind, I started the design of the POC app, keeping it as minimal as possible while still retaining all necessary functionality.

    POC V1

    The POC app aimed to show the integration of the scanning hardware with the iOS app, which would later run on an iPad. Upon completing and receiving a "Good" grade for facial scans, it would offer three recommendations that best matched the customer's skin tone.

    POC V2

    The second iteration of the POC app considered the customer's makeup preferences in its recommendations, and introduced basic features for active user feedback. It also provided increased visual aids for the users.

    View Prototype

    Hi-Fi Prototype

    After receiving approval for the POC app, I designed additional non-critical features to enhance the overall user experience. Finally, I incorporated YSL's design style guide and elevated the prototype to high-fidelity.

    View Prototype

    Reflection

    Impact

    The presence of an AI algorithm in-store gives Beauty Assistants greater credibility and instilled trust in customers. Without it, customers must rely solely on the BAs' judgment. Research indicates that with quantifiable data, customers are less likely to make returns and have increased confidence in their purchases.

    YSL E-Shade Finder is launched online, but the in-store counterpart has yet to be released.

    Challenges

    During this project, I had to overcome a couple of challenges. Being my first corporate experience, it was challenging navigating the different stakeholders involved, with so much internal politics. Nevertheless, I persevered with the help of my off-shore engineering team.
