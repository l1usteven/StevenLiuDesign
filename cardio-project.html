CLIENT

Card.io

MY ROLE

  • Founding UX Designer

SUMMARY

Card.io strived to gamify running, making it more accessible and engaging for players to seamlessly integrate into their daily routine

THE BRIEF

BUSINESS GOALS

Dynamic profile tab that actively highlights past routes, and personal bests.

PROBLEM

The current system has too many tabs, overwhelming users with information and making it unclear where to navigate.

RESEARCH PROCESS

METHODS USED

  • User Interviews
  • User Journey
  • Information Architecture
  • Market Research Analysis
  • A/B Testing
  • Prototype Testing

I interviewed players to identify their key preferences and optimized the information architecture based on their insights.

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

KEY FOCUS AREAS

  • What metrics do you consider the most important for tracking your running performance?
  • Are there any specific achievements or milestones you are proud of?
  • How do you use the information from past runs to set and achieve your fitness goals?
  • Is there anything you do not want displayed in your profile?

USER NEEDS

PERSONAL RECORDS

Some players feel overwhelmed by too many tabs and too much information. They want a simpler version that shows key stats and averages.

They want to be able to quickly glance at their past performances, with an option for a more in-depth breakdown of individual runs.

WEEKLY PERFORMANCE

Players also wish to track their progress over shorter time periods to assess their improvement.

The majority of players prefer a weekly roundup of their performances for the week.

PRIVACY IS KEY

Certain players prefer not to display specific personal information on their profiles but still want a public-facing profile for community interaction.

As the game tracks your current running position and route, prioritizing the safety of our players is crucial.
