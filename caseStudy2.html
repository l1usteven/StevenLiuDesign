Xbox App Redesign

Spec Project

User Experience Design| User Interface Design | Wireframing | Prototyping

Overview

Project Brief:

Xbox App Redesign: Design a new feature(s) for the existing brand that meets the business goals.

Time:

2 Weeks

The Problem:

Primary: Many users spend countless hours to get better at a game. After practicing and earning different statuses, titles, and or achievements, they still do not have a good way of displaying their hard work to their friends and community.

Secondary: Users that do not have friends to play games with online still want a way to engage with the community and see what other players are up to.

The Goal:

Objective was to identify a potential feature to implement that would solve a user pain point while maintaining the look and feel of the original brand.

Team:

3 Person Team Project

I worked in a cross-functional team that started out with 4 people but then ended up being 3 for the remainder of the design sprint.

Responsibilities:

User Flows, Site Mapping, Wireframing, Prototyping, Mock Up Design

Technologies Used:

Concepts (iOS), FigJam, Figma, Trello, Jira, Google Drive, Google Forms, Zoom

The Process

My process differs from project to project depending on different determining factors but not limited to:

Business needs, project goals, user needs, complexity, time and other constraints.

Research

The App Itself:

To start us off, our team simply downloaded the app from the AppStore and went through the process of signing up for an account. This allowed us to each have access to, as well as get crucial exposure to what the app already has to offer. We then would be able to start thinking about what features were lacking and how to design them to have the same look and feel of the existing user experience.

Functionality:

After going through and using the Xbox App, our team noticed that the Achievements and Social sections of the app were very limited in their functionality. We brainstormed collectively to come up with suitable survey and interview questions that would allow us to gain the most insight to how users utilize the Xbox app currently and what kind of difficulties they face. This allowed us to determine what kind of feature to implement in order to help the overall user experience.

User Surveys:

Our team sent out user surveys hoping to get an idea of what user tendencies are when it comes to a companion gaming app, as well as who is enough of a "gamer" to interview.

Do you track your in-game achievements?

Do you watch highlights/montage videos of games?

User Interviews:

Objective: To discover user habits around game achievements and how users engage with their online gaming community. This allowed us to determine pain points they might have while trying to share their achievements and with the app itself.

Research Synthesis:

With so much data gathered during the user interviews, my team organized everything in an affinity map to sort everything and make it easier to find trends. After analyzing the data, we created a persona that represented the target user. Meet, Caleb.

Persona:

Name: Caleb

Gender: Male

Occupation: Waiter

Archetype: Gamer

Bio:

Caleb is a single, goofy guy from LA, who enjoys playing games every chance he can. He loves to stream, as well as watch fellow gamers' streams. When he's not working his part-time job, he is at home in his loft trying to reach the newest achievements in his games.

Goals:

  • Track and share achievements to friends and sometimes to the community
  • Track friends' achievements
  • Keep up with the games he plays and their communities

Needs:

  • A way to check out his friends' achievements quickly and briefly

Pain Points:

  • He cannot share his achievements the way he wants to

Competitive & Comparison Analysis:

Our team did some features comparisons between some direct and indirect competitors to determine how the Xbox App stacked up against them. From the results, the app performed just as well if not better in most categories, with just a few falling short.

Key Takeaways:

  1. Lack of real gameplay footage
    • Keeping in mind that Xbox is still a business, having actual gameplay videos of each game would be beneficial.
  2. Unlike its competitors, the Xbox app does have game clips management
    • Even though this is the case, the system Xbox has is very basic.

Testing

After we finished iterating the prototype our team conducted usability and A/B testing to determine any other changes we should make before creating the high-fidelity prototype and mockups.

Usability Testing:

In order to conduct an effective usability testing scenario, Priscilla and I came up with 3 tasks and metrics that correspond with our main user flow.

Task 1: Find your most recent achievements and watch the 30 second clip of when you achieved it.
Task 2: Make one of your achievements public to viewers.
Task 3: Locate your friend's achievements and watch the most recent clip.

Metric 1: First task should be done within 2 minutes with zero mistakes.
Metric 2: Second task should be done in less than 1 minute with zero mistakes.
Metric 3: Third task should be doe within 3 minutes with no more than 1 mistake.

Key Findings:

Task 1: All 3 users had no problems with locating and watching the video clip of their achievement videos and finished within allotted time.

Task 2: All users found it was easy to navigate and find the public view toggle. However, 2/3 of the users felt the description for the toggle button was a bit vague and would prefer a simple "Public" and "Private" description.

Task 3: All 3 users struggled to navigate to find their friends' accomplishments due to misunderstood wording for titles or because they saw their friends' achievements in the achievements page.

Iteration:

With the usability test data in mind, our team quickly realized the flaws in our designs and understood where and how to go about making the necessary changes. Afterwards, we brought the design to a full mockup!
