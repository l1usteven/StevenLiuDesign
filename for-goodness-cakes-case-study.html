    Overview

    For Goodness Cakes is a custom cake business that creates scrumptious treats for all occasions. Saira, the self-taught baker, offers personalized cake creations and delivery services. She needs a landing page for her business to streamline communication with clients and manage orders efficiently.

    As a Project Manager, I supervised 3 designers and 5 developers in designing and developing a user-friendly landing page within budget and on time. The project spanned 2 months, with a 1-month design sprint followed by a 1-month implementation phase.

    Problems

    Custom Orders

    For Goodness Cakes needs a clear method of announcing updates and a streamlined platform for clients to place orders with precise specifications to avoid any potential confusion or disorganization.

    Solutions

    Bu

    Introduce Competition

    Incorporates a competitive aspect through the use of a ranked leaderboard, the ability to capture "turf," and rewards that can be earned.

    Community

    Establish Community

    Fostered a sense of community among users by offering features such as team creation, the ability to connect with friends, and exclusive in-store perks through the app.

    User Flow

    To understand the method in which players will access their statistics upon capturing "turf," a user flow was developed by analyzing the navigation and actions of players within the app.

    Card.io User Flow | Profile View Options

    Iterative Prototyping

    With a general understanding of what players needed in the profile and statistics screen, I experimented with several designs before finalizing one that incorporated all necessary requirements while maintaining ease of use.

    V1

    The initial design placed too much emphasis on statistical data, making it similar to other run tracking apps. This was revealed in A/B testing, where players did not perceive the app as a gamified version of a traditional running app.

    V2

    The second design introduced consistency throughout the app by incorporating a player HUD that would be visible on multiple screens. Additionally, the UX copy was adjusted to include more game-like elements, and a history log of past routes was added.

    V3

    The final version of this feature separated "History" and "Statistics" to give players access to their previous routes and a more in-depth view of their statistics. It also introduced "Teams" which allowed players to compete on a larger scale while feeling part of a community of individuals with a common goal of staying fit.

    Delivery

    I implemented a color-coded annotation system to facilitate the design handoff and make it smoother for the developer.

    Blue Dot: Represents the main frame and an overview explanation.

    Red Dot: Represents the important functions/animations for a feature/button.

    Green Dot: Additional information that is included for a screen that needs explanation but not critical for functionality.

    Reflection

    Impact

    As a former track athlete for 7 years, this project held a lot of personal significance for me. I fully understand the struggle to maintain motivation for fitness and running, especially during the COVID-19 pandemic. This app has the potential to greatly impact many people during these challenging times.

    Cardii.io has launched (2022) its beta version and is currently being enjoyed by players in 90 cities and 7 countries.

    Challenges

    During my time at Cardii.io, I encountered a common challenge faced by startup companies - the lack of funding. As a result, I was unable to continue with the team. However, the founders were determined and eventually achieved a successful launch. Despite my departure, I am proud to have been a part of their success and glad that I was able to contribute to it.
